Tech solves so many problems and makes life easier, but it also introduces new problems that we did not have to deal with before. Like science in general, it is [dual natured](https://scienceforthepeople.org/2018/04/12/dual-nature-of-science). I think that any proposal to research and develop new tech should take into consideration the problems that this new tech will introduce. For example face detection tech paves way for evil entities to enact evil on a much larger scale than they could do before.

Face detection tech is obviously a great tool that has so many wonderful applications. But it also makes it possible to mass-invade peoples' privacy at unprecedented scales. And the problem with tech like this is that it does not matter at how level you as an indivual choose to opt-out or disconnect, you can't escape it. There are so many cameras taking footage nowadays, that you can't go anywhere in public without being behind the lens of a camera.

Everywhere you go nowadays, there's probably someone streaming or taking pictures/videos with you in the frame. Furthermore, the public internet is filled with publically accessable cctv cameras. What happens when all those cameras can be used to follow people or pinpoint where and when a person is in the world.

Will we have maybe a google like search engine where you can search someone's face and you get a history of their movements. It's really scary and I think the scariest part is that it is so possible and within reach yet almost never talked about.

The larger issue is that up until a few years ago, a person has the option to opt out of privacy-intruding tech by just not using it. But I don't think that this is an option in the near-future, we will probably be exist into a world where it is hard (and getting harder) to keep any sense of privacy from ever so intrusive technologies.
