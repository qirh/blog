title: Information Diet
date: 2017-11-13
---

I have a very delicate relationship with information. I love to be informed about what is going on in the world in general and more specifically certain locations that I have a personal connection to. Most of this information is news or articles or essays or things like that.

However, I also think that the vast majority of news and published articles are simply fluff, they don't matter. While I think a lot of information that matters does not get that much attention.

I get most of my news from newsletters and rss feeds. I subscribe usually to 30-50 different outlets across these two streams. And I try to get through them every morning before my work day. I used to have a problem trying to get through everything because I was trying to look deep into too many stories or headlines. But now I think I found a happy medium in letting way more stories go than the ones that I actually will look into. For instance, I don't think keeping up with the latest updates on Brexit and the deal they're trying to work out is important to me, honestly I think it's a waste of time and energy. I just don't need to know to keep up with these things. I'm not saying they're not important, I'm saying that knowing about them at this level of detail for a person like me who is not involved in politics and has no influence on world affairs is pointless. But unfortunately, this is the majority of the news.

I think it's a lot more important to keep up to date with what's actually going on the world, which gets a lot less news coverage. Things like elections (or any other news like that) from places in the world I haven't heard of or been to are a lot more important to me than the latest trump rant on twitter. One of these things matter and are interesting, the other isn't.

In order to try and be as informed as I can, I do look at as many headlines as I can, because they give me a quick glimpse of what's going on, and then maybe lightly skim the article to see if there's anything more worth digging into. This way I get to have shallow knowledge about a lot of topics.

Then I try to save the actual reading to articles that I know I will remember in the future and I think will actually have an impact on the world. I think it's really funny to look back and try to read the news archives from 3 years ago. Most news organization now publish their archives and are accessible for free. So many pointless and meaningless stories are featured front and centre while some of the things that are actually important are harder to find.

Anyways, I'm still trying to figure things out, it's sometimes not so easy to be informed and up to date without having social media. But it is possible, and actually I feel like without social media it's easier to cut through the noise.
